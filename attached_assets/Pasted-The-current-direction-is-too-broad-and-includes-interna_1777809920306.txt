The current direction is too broad and includes internal tools that do not belong in this portal.

Rebuild/refocus the app as an applicant/employee-facing portal only.

The product name should be:

Occu-Med RDQA Resource Center

This portal is NOT for ExamQA analysts, clients, administrators, or internal case management. It is only for applicants and employees who received an RDQA notice and need help understanding the process and finding realistic, affordable resources to obtain the requested medical documentation.

Remove or hide:
- EQA Dashboard
- Client Dashboard
- Admin Template Library
- Smart Reply Generator
- Internal case assignment
- Analyst call scripts
- ROI calculator
- Client pricing tiers
- Internal readiness routing
- SME routing
- Any employer/client-facing analytics

The portal should focus on these applicant/employee goals:

1. Understand what RDQA means.
2. Understand why they received an RDQA notice.
3. Understand that RDQA does not automatically mean disqualification.
4. Understand what their provider needs to address.
5. Understand what documents to bring to their provider.
6. Find realistic, affordable medical care/testing resources.
7. Learn what questions to ask when calling providers.
8. Prepare for their appointment.
9. Avoid common documentation mistakes.
10. Know what to do if they cannot meet the deadline.

Important compliance positioning:
- Occu-Med is not providing medical advice.
- Occu-Med is not referring the applicant to a specific provider.
- Occu-Med is not endorsing or partnering with listed providers/resources.
- Occu-Med is not scheduling appointments.
- Applicants may choose any qualified provider.
- Resources are provided as a courtesy only.
- Applicants must verify pricing, availability, insurance acceptance, referral requirements, and services directly with each facility.
- The portal helps applicants understand the documentation requested in their RDQA letter.

What Is RDQA Page:
Explain in plain language:
- RDQA means additional information is needed.
- It gives the applicant/employee an opportunity to provide medical documentation.
- Occu-Med needs this information before issuing a final recommendation.
- The final employment decision remains with the employer.
- The process may involve follow-up with a healthcare provider of the applicant’s choice.
- The applicant is responsible for obtaining and submitting the requested information.

My Next Steps Page:
Create a step-by-step guide:
1. Read your RDQA letter.
2. Go to the page titled “Medical Information Requested.”
3. Identify each condition or finding listed.
4. Use the condition guide to understand what may be needed.
5. Gather your RDQA letter, job description, lab results, and medical records.
6. Contact a provider of your choice.
7. Ask the provider to address the specific items in the letter.
8. Submit the completed information to Occu-Med.
9. Contact Occu-Med if you cannot meet the deadline.

Create Resource Category Cards:
1. Community Health Centers / FQHCs
Best for:
- Uninsured or underinsured applicants
- Primary care visits
- Diabetes, blood pressure, basic chronic condition follow-up
Questions to ask:
- Do you offer sliding-scale pricing?
- Can a provider review my RDQA letter?
- Can the provider issue a written statement?

2. Hospital Financial Assistance / Charity Care
Best for:
- Expensive testing
- Imaging
- Cardiac testing
- Specialist-related services
Questions:
- Do you have financial assistance?
- Can I apply before scheduling?
- Are there separate facility or interpretation fees?

3. Cash-Pay Testing Options
Best for:
- Labs
- Imaging
- Some diagnostic testing
Questions:
- What is the cash-pay price?
- Is the written report included?
- Are there additional fees?

4. Dental Schools / Community Dental Clinics
Best for:
- DD2813 dental form
- Dental exams
- Lower-cost dental treatment
Questions:
- Can the dentist complete DD Form 2813?
- Can they identify dental category status?
- What is the cost for exam/x-rays?

5. Retail Clinics / Urgent Care
Best for:
- Basic visits
- BP readings
- Some labs
- Some EKGs
Questions:
- Can a provider issue a written statement?
- Can they review job functions?
- Are results available quickly?

6. University / Training Programs
Best for:
- Dental
- Respiratory therapy/PFT
- Vision/hearing
- Supervised low-cost services
Questions:
- Do you offer low-cost student clinic services?
- Are services supervised by licensed professionals?
- Can documentation be provided?

7. Transportation / Community Help
Best for:
- Applicants without transportation
Questions:
- Does 211 list medical transportation?
- Are there county transportation resources?
- Are there nonprofit ride programs?

Prepare for Appointment Page:
Checklist:
Bring:
- RDQA letter
- Medical Information Requested page
- Job description/essential job functions
- Lab results/testing
- Medication list
- Prior records
- Provider question sheet from the portal

Tell your provider:
- Please answer each item in the RDQA letter.
- Please review the job functions.
- Please focus on the specific role, not just general return-to-work.
- Please state whether restrictions or accommodations are needed.
- Please include relevant test results or medical history.

Common mistakes:
- Provider writes only “cleared to work”
- Provider does not address each bullet point
- Provider does not mention job functions
- Provider does not address restrictions/accommodations
- Provider omits testing results
- Provider omits diagnosis/current status
- Provider note is missing date/signature/credentials
